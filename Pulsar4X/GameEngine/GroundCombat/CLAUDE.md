# Ground Combat вҖ” Subsystem Reference

The planet-surface war layer: units you build, station in regions, move, and fight with вҖ” the ground echo of the space fleet/combat systems. Lives in `GameEngine/GroundCombat/`. **New 2026-07-04 (slice 5a).**

> **Read `docs/GROUND-COMBAT-MAP-DESIGN.md` first** вҖ” the locked design + the sub-slice roadmap (5aвҖ“5i). v1 target is **full tactical** (unit types + terrain + base-coverage + formations + a navigable click-to-place map), shipped as CI-gated sub-slices. This subsystem sits ON the region map (`Galaxy/PlanetRegionsDB` вҖ” the 4-slice ring per body).

---

## LOCKED PRINCIPLE вҖ” every buildable is a real building on the ground (2026-07-04)

The developer's rule: *"everything I build on a planet that's selectable in space is represented by an actual building on the planet itself."* The colony economy (`ComponentInstancesDB` installations) and this ground map are **two views of the SAME physical things** вҖ” nothing is abstract-only. So any new planet buildable must be a component (`CONVENTIONS.md` В§6) AND carry a **region location** so it draws on the planet view. The reconciliation is **done (#5, 2026-07-04):** `GroundInstallations.LocateColonyInstallations` (hooked in `ColonyFactory.CreateFromBlueprint`) drops a colony's installations into its capital region's `Region.InstallationIds`, so the planet view draws them and fortification counts them. (Follow-up: locate DYNAMICALLY-built installations вҖ” those added post-creation via the colony economy вҖ” at their install site; v1 covers the start colony's buildings.) Full rationale + slice mapping: `docs/GROUND-COMBAT-MAP-DESIGN.md` вҶ’ "LOCKED PRINCIPLE." This is *why* the infrastructure system exists: a located building can be bombarded, invaded, defended, and lost region-by-region; an abstract one can't be fought over.

## The model in one line

A planet body carries a **`GroundForcesDB`** (a roster of `GroundUnit`s). Each `GroundUnit` knows its **region**, its **owner faction**, and its **combat stats** вҖ” so one roster holds both sides of a contested world. Units are **built** at a colony through the normal industry rails (`GroundUnitDesign : IConstructableDesign`), **moved** regionвҶ’region on the region graph's crossing-time edges (5b), **fought** by a strength-math resolver mirroring the space `AutoResolve` (5c), and **captured** by flipping `FactionOwnerID` (5d) вҖ” the same primitive as a ship/fleet.

## Why units are DATA objects, not entities (v1)

**Environmental gear (E4) вҖ” the cradle-to-grave hazard counter.** A `GroundUnitDesign` carries an `EnvironmentalResistance` map (`HazardEffectType` вҶ’ 0..1 fraction negated); `RaiseUnit` snapshots it onto the `GroundUnit.EnvResistance` (like the combat stats), and the attrition step reduces incoming hazard damage by `unit.ResistanceTo(effect)`. This is the ground echo of a ship's `HazardResistanceAtb` вҖ” a heat-shielded design (`{HeatDamage:0.8}`) outlasts fire that kills an unprotected unit. **v1 folds the "gear" into the design** (its cost rides `ResourceCosts`); making gear a swappable, researched, *installable/strippable* component (the true grave rung) is the v2 promotion, alongside units-as-entities. Gauge: `GroundForcesTests.EnvironmentalGear_ReducesHazardAttrition` (0.8 вҶ’ 20% attrition, 1.0 вҶ’ immune, survives a clone).

A `GroundUnit` is a plain serializable object inside `GroundForcesDB`, not a full `Entity` вҖ” like `Galaxy.RegionFeature` inside `PlanetRegionsDB`. So a garrison of many units stays cheap and save-safe, and the combat resolver (5c) will read stats directly off the object. The trade-off: it can't reuse the Entity-based `AutoResolve.Resolve` verbatim вҖ” 5c **mirrors** that salvo-loop math over `GroundUnit`s instead. (If per-unit entities are ever needed вҖ” commanders, per-unit orders вҖ” that's a v2 promotion.)

## RESOLVED вҖ” ground-weapon ATTACK now costs (Option A + build-cost, byte-identical, 2026-07-11)

**The flagged free-Attack dial (below) is FIXED (S15).** It turned out to be fixable **byte-identically**, contradicting the "must re-baseline" assumption below. Two axes now cost:
- **Carry-weight (Option A, un-bypassable):** `GroundUnitAssembly.Compute` floors a weapon's effective carry-mass at `Math.Max(w.Mass, w.Attack * AttackCarryFactor)`. `AttackCarryFactor = 0.1` is the **tightest stock CarryMass/Attack ratio** (the claw's 2/20), so EVERY base-mod weapon sits at or below its dialed CarryMass вҶ’ byte-identical; only a heavier-hitting-than-stock design is floored up. A designer can no longer dial firepower to the ceiling and keep the weapon feather-light. (`GroundUnitAssemblyTests` вҖ” the stock Guardsman/autocannon gates вҖ” are the byte-identity tripwire.)
- **Build cost:** each ground-weapon JSON Mass formula adds `Max(0, Attack - <baseline>)*1`, so a harder-hitting weapon costs more materials to build (component `MassPerUnit` rises; anchored at each weapon's default Attack вҶ’ stock unchanged).

Base-mod demo: `default-design-heavy-rifle` (Attack 200) вҖ” +160 build-mass, and 20 carry-weight vs the stock rifle's 10. Gauge: `GroundWeaponAttackCostTests` (both axes; stock byte-identical). The historical framing + the options considered are kept below for context.

## (Historical) Flagged design question вҖ” ground-weapon ATTACK is a FREE dial (2026-07-11)

During the вҡҷ1 Weapons "cost the free dial" sweep (S9вҖ“S12 gave every SHIP direct-fire weapon's reach/velocity/damage dial a mass cost), the ground-weapon templates (`ground-rifle`/`ground-autocannon`/`ground-cannon`/`energy-weapon`/`claw-weapon` in `installations.json`) were found to have a **free `Attack` dial**: `Attack` feeds `GroundWeaponAtb.Attack` (the unit's firepower) but **nothing costs it** вҖ” the component `Mass` is `PropertyValue('CarryMass')`, and `CarryMass` is an INDEPENDENT player dial (default `10`, constant). So a designer can dial a rifle's Attack to the ceiling for zero carry-weight вҖ” a free-win dial the audit (`docs/REALISM-VS-GAMEPLAY-AUDIT.md`, CONVENTIONS В§16 transparency rule) says shouldn't exist.

**Why it wasn't fixed as a byte-identical data slice** (unlike the ship weapons): the ground carry-GATE reads `GroundWeaponAtb.Mass` (= the `CarryMass` ctor arg вҖ” see `GroundUnitAssembly.Compute`, `itemMass = a.Mass`), not the component `MassPerUnit`. So to make Attack cost carry-weight you must drive `CarryMass` from `Attack` вҖ” but `CarryMass` is a settable dial, so a formula default can be **overridden** (a designer re-opens the free win by dialing CarryMass low). A clean fix needs a **design decision** the developer should make:

- **Option A вҖ” Attack drives a carry-weight FLOOR** (engine): the assembler's per-item carry cost = `max(dialed CarryMass, f(Attack))`, so Attack always costs, un-bypassable. Small change in `GroundUnitAssembly` (the `itemMass` line); keeps CarryMass as a "base bulk" dial. *Recommended.*
- **Option B вҖ” remove the independent `CarryMass` dial**, make weapon mass a pure function of Attack (+ Range/Mode). Simpler mental model; loses the "same firepower, different bulk" design space.
- **Option C вҖ” a separate firepower gate** (like the P2b power / P2c ammo gates): "a frame can only bear so much attack." More systemic, more work.

All three touch the assembler (engine), not just JSON, and change the ground-unit *cost curve* вҖ” i.e. a deliberate rebalance that re-baselines the ground carry/mass gauges, NOT a byte-identical slice. So it was **deferred** rather than rushed. The ship side of the same principle is fully done (S9вҖ“S12). Pick an option before wiring.
