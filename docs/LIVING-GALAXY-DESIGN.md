# The Living Galaxy — the Objective (NOT a system)

**Status: an OBJECTIVE / BAR, not a build target.** A north-star statement in the family of `docs/NORTH-STAR-VISION.md` and `docs/MVP.md` — something every *other* system is measured against, never something you build. **Corrected 2026-07-07** after two earlier drafts wrongly tried to make it a system (an "event engine / surfacing filter / pacer"). That was the mistake this doc now exists to prevent.

---

## What it is

The Living Galaxy is the **emergent result of ALL our systems running together** — the ultimate objective of the whole design. A galaxy that **just IS**: the systems run, consequences accumulate on their own, and the player plays through whatever emerges. 250 in-game years with no alien contact are still full of rebellions, scientific discoveries, difficult choices, lost colonies, natural disasters, and civil upheaval — **because those things genuinely happen in a deep, interconnected simulation, not because anything makes them happen.**

## What it is NOT (the correction — read this first)

- **Not a system, engine, or layer.** There is nothing to build called "the Living Galaxy."
- **Not a storyteller / AI director** (the RimWorld model): no timer asking "has it been boring lately? better inject something."
- **Not an injector, pacer, filter, or check.** Nothing is forced, nothing is brought forward, nothing is topped up.

**Emergence cannot be built directly. It is what you GET when the parts are deep and connected enough.** Any attempt to build the emergence — an event engine, a surfacing filter, a pacer — is the director error in a new costume. Do not add one.

## How it is achieved (by building everything ELSE)

The Living Galaxy emerges when every *other* system is:

1. **Deep** — a real simulation with real pressures (morale decays, resources deplete, hazards strike, legitimacy erodes), not a shallow stat.
2. **Connected** — coupled to the others, so a shock in one ripples through the rest: legitimacy ← morale ← employment ← economy ← logistics ← population, with hazards + exploration + rivals + influence + espionage all poking the same web. **This coupling is the anti-flatline mechanism** — it is why the galaxy never fully settles and never needs a director to stir it. *The interconnection this whole design has been chasing IS what produces the living galaxy.*
3. **Observable + interactive** — the player can *see* what is happening and *respond*. **This is each system's OWN job** (its cradle-to-grave rung + the Visibility Gate), not a central layer's.

## The reattribution — where the real work actually goes

The legitimate work that the wrong drafts mis-filed under a phantom "engine" is real, but it belongs to the **individual systems**, each finishing its own visibility + interaction:

- Rebellion flips a flag silently today → that's the **rebellion system being unfinished** (it owes its player-facing surface, the reaction-window response, and the secession resolution). Finish it *there*.
- A disaster applies damage with no player moment → the **hazard system** owes its own surfacing.
- A discovery emits a one-line log entry → the **exploration system** owes its own moment/choice.

Done that way, the galaxy is alive with **no central machinery.** (Even the existing notification log is just each system reporting itself — not a "Living Galaxy" component.)

## The lens / bar it imposes on every system

Hold every system to this, alongside "earns its weight" (`REALISM-VS-GAMEPLAY-AUDIT.md`) and "fits the MVP scope" (`MVP.md`):

> **Does this system — built to depth, connected, and observable — contribute to a galaxy that lives on its own, without anything forcing it?**

If a system only produces drama when something pokes it, it is too shallow or too isolated — **deepen it or connect it; never prop it up with a director.**

## The balancing (the tricky part — and where it truly lives)

The balance between "too quiet" (a flatlined sim) and "too overwhelming" (everything on fire at once) is **the calibration of the individual systems' rates and couplings — not the tuning of a meta-layer:**

- **Rates** (decay / depletion / hazard frequency / thresholds) set how fast pressures build.
- **Coupling strength** sets how far a shock spreads (too tight = cascade/overwhelm; too loose = flatline).
- **Overwhelm is handled by the player's altitude + delegation**, never by making the sim produce less — an empire-CEO lets officers absorb the routine; a hands-on player drinks from the firehose by choice. Same "play at your own altitude" valve.

It is a **calibration problem, not a design problem** — it comes *last*, after the systems exist to be tuned, and it is done by watching gauges over long headless runs (rebellions / disasters / discoveries per century, and the player-attention cost). The steam-plant instinct: tune by the readings. Still not a system.

---

**Bottom line:** the Living Galaxy is the finish line, not a feature. We reach it by building every other system deep, connected, and observable — and by *refusing* to force it. When the parts are right, the galaxy lives on its own.
